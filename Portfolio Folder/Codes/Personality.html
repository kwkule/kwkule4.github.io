BUT WHY HIM?


Diogenes, also known as Diogenes of Sinope or the founder of cynicism, was a Greek philosopher born around 404 BC in Sinope, Anatolia. He was unlike other philosophers, in which, he was but a homeless beggar living inside of an old tub. Despite this, he was one of the most cocky/arrogant people I've ever heard of. He wasn't afraid to speak his mind despite his vulnerable living situation, and spouted some of the most absurd but humorous quotes in human history. Just a little anecdote about Diogenes: Alexander the Great upon hearing about him and his mad ramblings decided to take the time out his busy schedule to visit him because he was quite a fan of the madman. When Alexander asked if Diogenes desired anything, to which Diogenes replied (to the most powerful man in the world at the time, mind you) "Yes, I would like you to stand a little out of my sun." Absolute legend. I like this Diogenes quote in particular though because despite his demeanor and status, it showed that he was but a humble beggar.
